Luminis Foundation

Decentralized AI for Ecological Sovereignty

Mission

Luminis Foundation conducts scientific research in decentralized artificial intelligence, environmental sustainability, and privacy-preserving digital systems. Our work is rooted in the high-desert landscapes of Northern New Mexico, where we develop sovereign, ecologically-situated computing systems whose identity and inference are grounded in the biological state of a specific place—not dependent on cloud infrastructure. We pursue the long-term well-being of humanity and the natural world through public-benefit research and open knowledge.

Bridging Mycelium-Inspired Decentralized Computing and Symbiotic Plant–Fungal–AI Bio-Hybrid Systems in Northern New Mexico’s High-Desert Ecosystems

A synthesis of fungal electrophysiology, mycorrhizal ecology, and edge-AI engineering proposing bio-hybrid sensor–AI systems organized in mycorrhizal-inspired topologies for persistent ecological monitoring in piñon–juniper woodlands.
